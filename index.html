A
What is the difference between C3 and C4 plants in photosynthesis?
N
C3 Plants fix CO₂ directly in the Calvin cycle using RuBisCO, producing a 3-carbon compound (3-PGA) as the first stable product. They photorespire significantly, losing efficiency in hot, dry conditions.

C4 Plants have an additional CO₂ fixation step in mesophyll cells (producing 4-carbon oxaloacetate), then concentrate CO₂ in bundle sheath cells. This suppresses photorespiration and makes them far more efficient in high temperature and light.

Key NEET point: Sugarcane, maize, and sorghum are C4. Wheat, rice, and most trees are C3. C4 plants have Kranz anatomy — look for this in questions.
